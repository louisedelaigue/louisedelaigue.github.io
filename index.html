about

I am currently involved in a joint PhD programme with the NWO-NIOZ Royal Netherlands Institute for Sea Research and Utrecht University, under Dr. Matthew Humphreys.

Education

2016
BSc, Concordia University, Department of Geography, Planning and Environment (Montreal, CA)

Environmental Science student, with an internship at the South African Shark Conservancy (conservation and sustainable utilization of marine resources through research, education and outreach, collection and sample processing, marine debris analysis, shark tagging and lab work).

MSc, McGill University, Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences (Montreal, CA)

Using geochemical tracers and optimization algorithms, my Masters’ thesis consisted of analyzing the water masses present in the Saguenay Fjord and their circulation, as well as the gaseous exchange taking place at the air-sea interface. Supervised by Dr. Alfonso Mucci.

2018
2020 -
PhD Student, Royal Netherlands Institute for Sea Research & Utrecht University (Texel, NL)

Ocean-going, observational carbonate chemistry. My research focuses on how the ocean responds to increasing anthropogenic CO2 emissions, with an emphasis on under-sampled regions such as the southern Indian Ocean and Pacific Ocean.
